# Anthropic, AI, national security, and the values

After Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth designated Anthropic as a supply chain risk, a designation historically reserved for foreign adversaries such as Russian cybersecurity firms and Chinese chip suppliers, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei sat down with CBS News for a candid interview.

What is the core issue? Anthropic established two strict boundaries on how its AI can be used by the U.S. military:
+ **Domestic mass surveillance** — AI now enables the aggregation and analysis of data on American citizens at a rate that outpaces current law. According to Amodei, technology has advanced faster than legal interpretations of the Fourth Amendment.

+ **Fully autonomous weapons** — Systems that operate without any human involvement. His concern isn't theoretical, but practical: current AI models aren't reliable enough, and accountability frameworks don't yet exist.

What struck me most was Amodei's framing. Anthropic wasn't portrayed as anti-military. In fact, they were the first AI company to deploy models on classified cloud infrastructure and develop custom models for national security applications. They support 98–99% of Pentagon use cases. The dispute centers on the remaining 1%.

His argument: **"We have to find a way to win in a way that preserves our values. We can't just be a total race to the bottom."**

On the business impact, Amodei was direct — Anthropic will be fine. He noted the designation's actual legal scope is far narrower than public statements suggested, and called the broader framing a deliberate attempt to create "fear, uncertainty, and doubt."

His long-term solution? Congress needs to catch up with where AI technology is going. Private companies shouldn't be the final arbiters of these decisions — but until legislation exists, someone has to hold the line.

💡 Whether you agree with Anthropic's position or not, this moment raises a question every company building powerful technology will eventually face: **at what point does a vendor's ethical framework override a customer's instructions?**
