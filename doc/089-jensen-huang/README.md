# Jensen Huang on the Lex Fridman Podcast

In his latest conversation with Lex Fridman, NVIDIA's CEO offered a rare glimpse into the mind of someone who doesn't just predict the future; he engineers it.

**On bold bets.** When NVIDIA introduced CUDA to the GeForce line, it nearly destroyed the company's profit margins. Its market capitalization dropped from approximately $8 billion to $1.5 billion. Jensen did it anyway because he had already reasoned his way to an inevitable outcome. That bet became the foundation of the entire AI computing era.

**On leadership.** He doesn't do one-on-one meetings. With over 60 direct reports, every problem is addressed collectively. "No conversation is ever just one person." This is an extreme example of co-design applied to company culture itself.

**On scaling.** Jensen sees four compounding laws driving AI forward: pre-training, post-training, test-time compute, and agentic scaling. The bottleneck isn't data anymore. It's computing power. Moreover, token costs are dropping by an order of magnitude each year.

**On the future of work.** "Every carpenter will be a coder." AI doesn't replace purpose; it automates tasks. For example, the radiologist workforce grew after AI became better than humans at reading scans. This pattern will repeat itself across industries.

**On resilience.** "How hard can it be?" He admits that this question gets him into trouble, but it also enables him to build things that no one thought possible.

💡 NVIDIA isn't manufacturing chips anymore. Instead, it's building AI factories on a planetary scale.
